One of the less remembered movies from the Disney canon of movies is 2014's 
[Big Hero 6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Hero_6_(film)): A reinvention
of the Marvel Comics superhero team of the same name. I did not see this
movie when it came out, when I was almost at the edge of the target
market for this. I did end up seeing it when my 6-year-old insisted
on watching it when it popped up on [Disney+](https://www.disneyplus.com/)
and was frankly blown away.

> [!WARNING]
> Spoilers Ahead

The movie is the origin story of the eponymous team of superheroes, led
by the plucky young inventor and super genius Hiro Hamada. Hiro is your
_almost-but-not-quite_ stereotypical child genius, who lives with his brother
and aunt, and gets into trouble. His brother is able to get him interested
in applying to the prestigious university he is a student of himself. However,
a series of events leads to the brother dying and the subsequent quest for
vengeance ends up creating the eponymous team.

The movie is absolutely gorgeous. It's hard to overstate how beautiful
the fictional city of _San Fransokyo_ looks, and how expressive the faces
and the body language of the characters is. On top of that, the story pulls
at your heartstrings, both employing and deconstructing the classical
tropes. There's the [_Wise Mentor_](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/MentorArchetype),
contrasted with the designated [Corrupt Corporate Executive](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/CorruptCorporateExecutive).

There's the classic [Five-Man Band](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/FiveManBand) with all five roles
clearly assigned.

However, the film does not shy away from subverting the very same tropes. The [Sixth-Ranger](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/SixthRanger) is a binding
force, rather than a disruptive force. There is a [revenge quest](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/Revenge), but it is 
contrasted with another [revenge quest that is executed differently](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/RoaringRampageOfRevenge).

This latter quest of revenge is what I want to talk about today.

## The Callaghan Affair

Professor Callaghan is a major character in the movie. He shows up early as the
mentor for Hiro's brother, and encourages our protagonist to apply to the
university. He is also one of the judges at the technology showcase where Hiro
demonstrates his revolutionary microbots before seemingly dying in an explosion.

However, the twist is that the primary antagonist in the movie, the unnamed
_yokai_, is actually Professor Callaghan, who faked his death to gain control
of the microbots for his own (arguably) nefarious purposes.
